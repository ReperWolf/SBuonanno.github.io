Space Traders and Raiders

COM 429 Professional Game Production

Overview

The main purpose of this class was to simulate operating as a game development company and the professor would be our "client" who approached us with a GDD he created. This GDD was based on a boardgame concept he designed and wanted us to turn it into a variant of his concept. Each student had to decide what department they wanted to be in and if they wanted to be head of that department. The overall timeline was to create a revised GDD, alpha, beta and deliverable builds within 1 semester or roughly 4 months. In order to stay consistent with deadlines and meetings we used SCRUM during the entire production. Collectively, the entire class agreed to maintain the original concept as best as we could and decided to create a virtual turn-based 2D sci-fi strategy game.

Game Concept

While reviewing the original GDD it has a very similar feel to Stellaris and Sid Meier's Civilizaiton games, so we decided to create the game with similar atmoshpere. There are 4 races within the game each having their own unique cultural background and allows up to 4 people to play. Each player starts out with 1 home system and ship with the goal of expanding their empire across the galaxy. Once players reach a certain point in the game they will either have to choose, become a trader or raider. The path that the player chooses will determine what unique set of rules the player must follow to then win the game.

Trader Rules
  • Able to control more than one system.
  • Allowed to trade with other traders with the benefit of not having any restirctions on trade. Traders can custom trade anything and are not restricted to fixed pricing.
  • NOT allowed to initiate combat.
Raider Rules
  • Allowed to initiate combat.
  • Able to hold more minerals on ships that count towards their victory conditions.
  • Can raid systems and farm the resources from destroyed ships.
Role: Lead Artist

I decided on the Lead Artist role because I wanted to understand the coordination of artists being able to produce assets together and how that corrolates with the other departments as a whole. Within the 'Art Department' there were 8 artists including myself and I had the challenge of delegating tasks to each student who all have varying schedules. In addition to being Lead Artist I assisted in conflicts within the class and organized meetings associated with the Art Department along with separate meetings including the producer and leads.

Once tasks were established I had all artists create their own workloads including myself. This was the optimal choice to help balance workloads and have artists select tasks that interested them. To also help assist keeping the department on track with deadlines, work completion and preventing burnout I created some guidelines:

  1. Art Department meetings will be held once a week outside of class to stay up to date on what is completed, in progress and any issues that need to be resolved.
  2. Select the tasks that you are confident in completing in a timely manner.
  3. If all your tasks are completed ahead of time and with approval I will assign you to assist any fellow artist that are requesting assistance.
Software and Design

The chosen style of the game was pixel art since assets can be created fairly easily without any prior experience. Aseprite is a pixel art design program that can be bought through Steam and that was the program I used for my assets. Some of the artists didn't want to purchase Aseprite so I found an alternative, which I have used in the past call Piskel. Piskel is a free pixel art program that is web-based and only requires creating an account. The project was built using the game engine Unity because all of our programmers were most familiar with it. Below are the assets I created for this project:

Fungi Race

The Fungi race has a fleet that looks like a collection of different ships from the other races, this is because their biology as a race is intertwined with technology. Because of their biology and adaptability it allows them to take control over technology that is foreign to them and create it as their own.

Hive Race

Similar to bees the Hive race has a queen who is bigger than the rest and has a hunger for conquest. The ships for the Hive represent the combination of technology and their architectural structure that looks similar to insects from Earth.
